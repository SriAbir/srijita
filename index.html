About

I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Software Engineering Unit at Chalmers University of Technology and the University of Gothenburg, where I study how autonomous AI agents can make safety-critical software more secure. My current work designs role-based, multi-agent workflows that detect, analyse, and help remediate software vulnerabilities, with a particular focus on connected and automotive systems.

Over more than nine years I have followed a single thread — the security and verification of complex software — through several domains. My Master's and doctoral research developed blockchain-based smart contracts and formal security models for cloud computing, and I later led two DRDO-funded projects on policy verification and software-defined security for SDN and NFV infrastructures. That trajectory now converges on agentic AI for secure software engineering.

My research is closely tied to industry. Through the Software Center and SFO Transport I collaborate with partners including Volvo, Scania, Grundfos, and Ericsson, bringing academic ideas to bear on real engineering problems. I am currently on the academic job market, seeking a faculty position where I can build an independent research program and supervise doctoral students. Outside research I enjoy music, films, and books, and I speak Bengali, English, Hindi, and some German.

Research

Agentic AI for Secure Software Engineering Current

I design role-based and multi-agent (Crew-AI) architectures that triage, analyse, and remediate software vulnerabilities, and I study how AI agents collaborate — and miscommunicate — when given real software-engineering tasks.

Automotive Software Security

Mapping the vulnerability landscape of connected vehicles through architectural studies of the AUTOSAR Adaptive Platform and automotive system-on-chip software, carried out with partners in the Swedish transport sector.

Blockchain & Cloud Security

My doctoral work built blockchain-based smart contracts and formal models for decentralised policy enforcement, secure cloud data location and deletion, and virtual-machine image integrity using Hyperledger and Ethereum.

Software-Defined Network Security DRDO-funded

As a research lead I built tools that verify security-policy compliance across SDN and NFV infrastructures, together with a software-defined security framework for managing network and security state dynamically.

Teaching

I teach in a student-centred, constructivist style: I see myself as a facilitator rather than a lecturer, lean on problem-based learning and frequent formative feedback, and pay close attention to inclusivity and to supporting women in STEM. Across five years as an Assistant Professor and through my current postdoc, I have taught widely across the computer-science curriculum and supervised Bachelor's and Master's theses from proposal through defence.

A distinctive part of my teaching is hands-on work with embedded and real-time systems on a live Volvo truck platform, including student hackathons in which teams programmed the vehicle directly. I also design courses around professional tools — version control with GitHub and learning-management workflows in Canvas — so students learn collaboration practices, not just programming.

Courses taught: Software Evolution · AI & Cybersecurity · Development of Embedded and Real-Time Systems · Cloud Computing · Information & Network Security · Database Management Systems · Data Structures & Algorithms · Digital Electronics · Information Theory & Coding · Object-Oriented Programming in Java
